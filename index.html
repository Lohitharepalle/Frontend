Taj Mahal, India

The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being “the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage”.

Eiffel Tower, Paris

Its height and unique silhouette floating above the Paris landscape quickly made the Eiffel Tower one of the most popular attractions in Paris. The Tower's destiny is closely linked to that of the city of Paris, the capital of France and the Eiffel Tower's owner.

Machu Picchu, Peru

At a dizzying 2,430 meters above sea-level, the view form Machu Picchu on a clear day is breath taking. The famous ruins are surrounded by towering peaks apus, which means “sacred peaks” in Quechua and have views of the surrounding valley.

Venice, Italy

With its Venetian Gothic architecture, Renaissance and Baroque buildings, wide piazzas, and narrow canals, Venice is often described as one of the most beautiful cities in the world.

Cherry Blossom Season, Japan

It symbolizes both life and death, beauty and violence. As the coming of spring promises new life, so the blooming of cherry blossoms brings a sense of vitality and vibrancy. At the same time, their short lifespan is a reminder that life is fleeting.

Banff National Park, Canada

Home to sparkling lakes, snow-capped mountains and fascinating wildlife, the area attracts visitors from around the world. Summers offer amazing outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking.

Tamil Nadu, India

Ooty is a beautiful hill station in Tamil Nadu, known for its misty mountains, tea plantations, and colonial architecture. Mahabalipuram is a coastal town in Tamil Nadu famous for its ancient rock-cut temples, stunning sculptures, and scenic beaches.

Iguazu Falls, Argentina

Iguazu Falls in Argentina is a stunning sight of huge waterfalls surrounded by lush forests. It's a magnificent natural wonder that shows the amazing power and beauty of nature.

Great Pyramid of Giza, Egypt

The pyramids of Giza are known for their remarkable engineering, as they were built thousands of years ago using massive limestone blocks, precise construction, and enduring mysteries surrounding their purpose and construction techniques.
